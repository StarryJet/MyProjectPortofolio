Diagnosis

Based on the patient's symptoms and examination, the provisional diagnosis is Tension-Type Headache (TTH), likely exacerbated by stress and poor sleep hygiene. There is no evidence of an underlying systemic illness or infection at this time.

Treatment

Immediate Recommendations:

  • Over-the-counter pain relievers such as ibuprofen (200-400 mg) as needed, not exceeding 1200 mg per day.
  • Ensure adequate hydration and balanced nutrition.

Lifestyle Modifications:

  • Improve sleep hygiene: Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep per night, maintain a consistent sleep schedule, and create a restful sleeping environment.
  • Stress management techniques: Engage in regular physical activity, practice mindfulness or relaxation exercises, and consider counseling services offered by the university if needed.
  • Limit caffeine and alcohol intake, especially in the late afternoon and evening.

Follow-Up:

  • Schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks to reassess symptoms and adjust the treatment plan if necessary.
  • If symptoms worsen or new symptoms arise (e.g., significant vision changes, severe headache, or vomiting), seek immediate medical attention.

Note: If the patient experiences no significant improvement or if symptoms recur frequently, a referral to a neurologist for further evaluation may be considered.
